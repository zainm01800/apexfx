How APEX works

APEX is an honesty-first research tool, not a crystal ball. It reads the market the way a disciplined desk would — many independent angles, weighed against each other, with the risks given equal weight — and it keeps an honest, public score of how often it's actually right. Here's exactly what happens when you hit Deep Analyse, in plain language.

The pipeline

1

Gather the evidence

Price history across timeframes, technicals, fundamentals & accounting quality (for stocks), macro / intermarket signals, positioning, seasonality, and — for crypto — perp funding, open interest, ETF flows and on-chain data.

2

A committee of specialist AI analysts debate it

Separate technical, fundamental, macro and risk perspectives are formed, then debated — and the final verdict is produced by an ensemble of different AI models, not one. When they disagree, confidence drops and the verdict leans toward WAIT. Disagreement is treated as a signal, not papered over.

3

An independent quant engine checks it

A separate statistical engine detects the market regime, sizes risk with a capped fractional-Kelly rule (and can veto a trade), and runs the setup through proper overfitting validation — combinatorial purged cross-validation, the Deflated Sharpe Ratio and Probability of Backtest Overfitting. Most strategies fail this on purpose — that's the system working.

4

The confidence is calibrated against our own record

A raw "78%" from an AI means little. APEX re-maps the displayed confidence using its realised hit-rate — so the number reflects how often calls at that level have actually worked, scored with a Brier score and reliability curve. It also flags its own weak inputs (e.g. ICT/SMC, COT, seasonality) rather than dressing them up as edge.

5

You get a verdict + a real trade plan

BUY / SELL / WAIT, a calibrated confidence, an expected-value framing, a pre-mortem ("if this loses, why?"), and concrete entry / stop / take-profit levels tailored to your chosen trade style.

Why you can trust the number

Every resolved call — win or loss — is logged to a public, timestamped record. We show the losses competitors hide, because the only honest measure of an analysis is whether its stated confidence holds up over many calls.

What APEX does NOT do

  • It does not predict the future. No tool can. Our edge is risk management, regime awareness and honest validation — not fortune-telling.
  • It is not financial advice. It's research and education. Every decision and every loss is yours.
  • It doesn't claim a win rate it can't show. If the public record isn't good, you'll see that too.
  • The AI is a reasoning layer, not a price oracle. Language models can't forecast markets; we use them to weigh evidence transparently, and lean on the statistical engine for rigour.
  • Data can be delayed. On the free data feed, intraday prices may lag ~15 minutes — best suited to swing and position trades.
