Welcome to APEXFX
A professional-grade trading terminal running entirely in your browser. Use this guide to master every feature — from chart reading to AI-powered analysis and trade journalling.
📊 Live Charts
Multi-timeframe candlestick charts with volume, RSI, MACD, Stochastics and custom indicators.
🤖 AI Intelligence
Pattern recognition, support/resistance detection, trend strength analysis and AI trading signals.
⌕ AI Scanner
One-click scanner prompts — filter by RSI, volume, bullish/bearish setups, candlestick patterns and more.
📓 Trade Journal
Log trades with entry/exit prices, R:R, emotions, screenshots and performance analytics.
✏️ Drawing Tools
Trend lines, Fibonacci retracements, horizontal rays, rectangles, pitchforks, text labels and more.
⚖ Position Sizer
Calculate position size based on account size, risk %, entry and stop-loss levels.
Use the left navigation to jump to any feature section. All features are described with step-by-step instructions.
App Layout
APEXFX is divided into five main zones. Understanding the layout helps you navigate quickly.
Zone Map
🔝 Top Bar
Symbol selector · live price · OHLCV · timeframes · chart type · indicators button · Replay · Screenshot · Settings · Help.
⌕ Scanner Bar
Just below the top bar. Drag/scroll the prompt chips to run AI scans instantly. Filter by typing in the search box.
◀ Left Sidebar
Watchlist of symbols with live prices & % change. Switch to Alerts tab to manage price alerts.
✏️ Drawing Toolbar
Thin vertical strip to the left of the chart. All drawing and annotation tools live here.
📊 Chart Area
Main canvas + optional sub-panels (Volume, RSI, MACD, Stoch). Scroll to zoom, drag to pan.
▶ Right Panel
Four tabs: AI Analysis · Trade Journal · Analytics · News Feed. Drag the resize handle to adjust width.
Both sidebars are resizable. Drag the thin amber resize handle between panels. Double-click or click the collapse arrow to hide a panel entirely and maximise chart space.
Symbols & Search
Load any stock, crypto, forex pair or futures contract into the chart.
How to Change Symbol
1
Click the symbol button in the top-left of the top bar (shows current symbol like AAPL · NASDAQ).
2
Type to search — the list filters as you type by symbol or company name.
3
Click any result to load it. The chart, price and OHLCV display update immediately.
Watchlist Quick-Load

Click any symbol row in the left sidebar Watchlist to load it onto the chart instantly. The active symbol is highlighted with an amber left border.

Symbols in the watchlist show live bid prices and colour-coded % change (green = up, red = down).
Timeframes
Switch between seven timeframes to analyse price action at different granularities.
1m
1-minute bars. Best for scalping and very short-term entries.
5m
5-minute bars. Popular for intraday trading.
15m
15-minute bars. Good for intraday swings and confirmation.
1H
1-hour bars. Ideal for day traders looking at broader structure.
1D
Daily bars. Default view. Best for swing trading and trend analysis.
1W / 1M
Weekly & Monthly bars. Use for macro trend and key level identification.
A pro approach: always check a higher timeframe first (e.g. 1D) for trend direction, then drop to a lower timeframe (1H or 15m) for entry timing.
Chart Types
Four chart styles are available. Click the style icons in the top bar to switch.
▪ Candlestick
Default. Each bar shows Open, High, Low, Close. Green body = close > open (bullish). Red body = close < open (bearish). Best for reading price action and patterns.
∿ Line
Plots only the closing price. Cleaner view, good for spotting overall trend direction without noise.
◿ Area
Like a line chart but with filled area beneath. Gives a strong visual of price level and trend.
⬡ Heikin Ashi
Smoothed candlesticks that filter noise. Consecutive same-colour candles indicate strong trend continuation. Reversals become easier to spot.

Fibonacci & Shapes
Advanced drawing tools for identifying trade setups.
Fibonacci Retracement
1
Select the FIB tool (keyboard F).
2
For an uptrend: click the swing LOW first, then drag to the swing HIGH.
3
For a downtrend: click the swing HIGH first, then drag to the swing LOW.
4
Retracement levels are drawn automatically: 23.6% 38.2% 50% 61.8% 78.6%
The 61.8% level (the "golden ratio") is considered the strongest retracement support/resistance zone.
Pitchfork

An Andrews Pitchfork requires three pivot points. Click P1 (first swing), P2 (subsequent high), P3 (subsequent low). The tool draws a median line and two parallel channel lines. Price tends to gravitate towards the median line.

Rectangle Zones

Use rectangles to mark supply and demand zones, consolidation ranges, or value areas. Click and drag to define the box boundaries on price and time.

Built-in Indicators
Click ☰ Indicators in the top bar to open the indicator library and toggle indicators on or off.
Sub-Panel Indicators
📊 Volume
Bar chart below the main chart. Green bars = up-close volume, red = down-close. High volume confirms price moves; low volume warns of weak moves.
📈 RSI (14)
Relative Strength Index. Oscillates 0–100. Above 70 = overbought. Below 30 = oversold. Watch for divergence with price for reversal signals.
📉 MACD (12,26,9)
Moving Average Convergence Divergence. Signal line crossovers indicate momentum shifts. Histogram shows the difference between MACD and signal lines.
〰 Stochastic (14,3)
Oscillator comparing close to range over 14 periods. Above 80 = overbought, below 20 = oversold. %K/%D crossovers signal entries.
Overlay Indicators
SMA / EMA
Simple and Exponential Moving Averages overlaid on the main chart. Common periods: 9, 20, 50, 200. Price above MA = bullish bias.
Bollinger Bands
Two standard deviation bands around a 20-period MA. Squeeze = low volatility / potential breakout. Touch of outer band = extension / mean-reversion signal.
VWAP
Volume Weighted Average Price. Widely used by institutions. Price above VWAP = bullish intraday, below = bearish.
Remove any sub-panel indicator by clicking the ✕ button in its top-right corner.
Custom Indicator Builder
Build your own indicators using a formula editor. Access it via ☰ Indicators → Build Custom.
Steps to Build a Custom Indicator
1
Name your indicator — give it a descriptive name like "My EMA Crossover".
2
Choose display type — Overlay draws it on the main chart; Sub-panel draws it in a separate panel below.
3
Write your formula in the formula box. Use Quick Insert chips to add functions faster.
4
Set colour, line style and width using the style controls.
5
Click ▶ Test to validate the formula, then ✚ Add to Chart.
Available Sources

close open high low volume

Available Functions

sma(source, period) ema(source, period) rsi(source, period) stdev(source, period) highest(source, period) lowest(source, period) abs(x) sqrt(x)

AI Analysis Panel
The 🤖 AI tab in the right panel analyses the current chart in real time and provides structured intelligence.
🔍 Pattern Recognition
Detects candlestick and chart patterns on the current symbol. Each pattern shows a confidence bar and bullish/bearish/neutral badge.
🏗 Support & Resistance
Automatically identifies key S/R levels from recent price history. These are plotted on the chart and listed in the panel.
📡 AI Signal
A natural-language summary of the current technical setup — bullish, bearish or neutral — with reasoning.
📐 Trend Strength
Measures the strength and direction of the current trend using multiple factors.
⚡ Volatility & Range
Shows current ATR, daily range statistics and relative volatility context.
🎯 Nearest Levels
Displays the nearest support and resistance price levels above and below current price.
The AI panel updates whenever you change symbol or timeframe. Use it alongside your own analysis — never as the sole basis for a trade decision.

Trade Journal
Log every trade, track performance and review your history. Access via the 📓 Journal tab in the right panel.
Logging a Trade
1
Click + New to open the trade log modal.
2
Fill in Symbol, Direction (Long/Short), Entry price, Stop Loss, Take Profit and Date.
3
Add optional fields: Setup name, Notes, Emotional state and Tags (e.g. Breakout, Reversal, News).
4
Attach a chart screenshot — click the 📷 button or use the "Capture Chart" option.
5
Save the entry. It appears in your journal list with P&L and R:R calculated automatically.

Bar Replay
Practice trading on historical data without risking real money. Bar Replay hides future bars and lets you step through price history one candle at a time.
How to Use Bar Replay
1
Click ⏮ Replay in the top bar (or press R) to enter Replay mode.
2
The chart will mask all candles after a starting point. You'll see a replay control bar appear.
3
Use ▶ Play to auto-advance bars, or step forward one bar at a time.
4
Practice making trading decisions, drawing levels and placing annotations as if trading live.
5
Click ⏮ Replay again to exit and return to the live chart view.
Bar Replay is one of the most powerful tools for improving chart reading skill. Use it daily on different symbols and timeframes.
Position Size Calculator
Calculate the correct position size for any trade based on your risk parameters. Click the ⚖ button in the top bar (or press P).
How to Use
1
Enter your Account Size (e.g. $10,000).
2
Set your Risk % — the percentage of your account you're willing to lose on this trade (e.g. 1%).
3
Enter your Entry Price and Stop-Loss Price.
4
The calculator outputs the number of shares/units to trade so your max loss equals exactly your risk amount.
Never risk more than 1–2% of your account on a single trade. Consistent position sizing is the foundation of long-term trading survival.
