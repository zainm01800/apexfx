# Sub-daily trading after costs: where the evidence says it can survive

**Bottom line up front.** The documented sub-daily edges that are real are also thin: ~2–10 bps of gross edge per trade. They survive only where round-trip cost ≤ ~5 bps and the traded interval's range is ≥ 10–20× cost. That rules out 15m/1h FX scalping entirely, marginalizes gold CFDs, and leaves exactly three defensible sub-daily candidates: **BTC/ETH US-close momentum at low-fee venues, equity-index last-30-min momentum on futures (not wide CFDs), and USD fix-flow trades at the London close** — plus a daily-frequency crypto/FX momentum anchor. Details and sources below.

---

## 1. Crypto intraday momentum / autocorrelation — the strongest sub-daily lead

**The core paper:** Shen, Urquhart & Wang (2022, *Financial Review* 57(2):319–344) define a BTC "trading day" from the volume-spike open to 17:00 EST (CME futures break), using tick data from Bitfinex, Bitstamp, CEX.IO, Coinbase, Kraken (to end-2020). Findings, from the [accepted manuscript (CentAUR, U. Reading)](https://centaur.reading.ac.uk/100181/3/21Sep2021Bitcoin%20Intraday%20Time-Series%20Momentum.R2.pdf):

- First half-hour predicts last half-hour: slope 0.968, Newey-West t = 4.38, R² = 1.44% (2.12% with the second-to-last half-hour, which enters *negatively* — anti-persistence). Out-of-sample R²OOS = 1.09–1.61%, *higher* than Baltussen et al.'s bond/commodity/currency futures.
- Effect is concentrated in **high-volume and high-volatility days** (R² = 3.86% on high-volume days; *insignificant* on low-vol days) and when the first-half-hour move is positive.
- Gross timing strategies: 7.8%/yr (first-half-hour signal), 17.3%/yr (second-to-last, reversed), 16.7%/yr combined, vs 6.5%/yr always-long; success rates >50%; Sharpe up to 1.15 in the asset-allocation version (CER 8.09%/yr). Works *best in BTC bear years* (2014, 2015, 2018) — a hedging property.
- **The honest number — breakeven transaction costs: 3, 7, and 10 bps per trade** for the three strategies unlevered. At Bitstamp's 25 bps taker fee the strategies are explicitly **not profitable**; at 10:1 leverage (margin) breakevens scale to 29/64/96 bps. So the raw edge is **~3–10 bps/trade** — real but fee-fragile.
- Mechanism tests reject late-informed trading (news-count interaction insignificant) and support **liquidity provision** (intraday traders' disposition effect + overnight-risk aversion), consistent with [Elaut et al. (2018)](https://biblio.ugent.be/publication/8060014) in FX.

**Corroboration:** [Wen, Bouri, Xu & Zhao (2022, *North American Journal of Economics & Finance* 62)](https://ideas.repec.org/a/eee/ecofin/v62y2022ics1062940822000833.html) — hourly BTC returns (5-min data, 2013–2020) show **both intraday momentum and intraday reversal** (reversal is crypto-specific, attributed to overreaction/overconfidence); patterns shift around jumps, FOMC announcements, liquidity regimes, and COVID; also present in ETH, LTC, XRP; timing strategy beats always-long and buy-and-hold.

**Frequency-vs-cost in crypto TA:**
- [Corbet et al. (2019)](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S1059056024003010) (as summarized in a later *Int. Rev. Econ. Fin.* paper): MA rules most profitable at **1-min** frequency — gross.
- [Svogun & Bazán-Palomino (2022, *J. Int. Fin. Markets, Inst. & Money* 79)](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S1042443122000816): once transaction costs are charged on both 1-min and daily rules, **daily-frequency rules are more robust**; 1-min profitability erodes.
- Daily/weekly horizon (not sub-daily but the natural anchor): [Liu & Tsyvinski (2021, *RFS* 34(6):2689–2727)](https://ideas.repec.org/a/oup/rfinst/v34y2021i6p2689-2727..html) document a **strong time-series momentum effect** in crypto plus investor-attention predictability; [Hudson & Urquhart (2021, *Annals of Operations Research* 297)](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10479-019-03357-1) run ~15,000 technical rules on BTC and three other cryptos with higher risk-adjusted returns than buy-and-hold; [Gerritsen et al. (2020, *Finance Research Letters* 34)](https://ideas.repec.org/a/eee/finlet/v35y2020ics1544612319308025.html) find BTC MA rules profitable after costs.

**Robustness caveats (be candid in the pre-registration):**
- Nearly all samples end 2018–2020. [Petukhina, Reule & Härdle (2021, *European Journal of Finance* 27(1–2):8–30)](https://ideas.repec.org/a/eee/ecofin/v62y2022ics1062940822000833.html) ("Rise of the machines?") document that algo/HFT entry changed crypto intraday patterns; [Köchling, Müller & Posch (2019, *Economics Letters* 174)](https://ideas.repec.org/f/c/pro1069.html) show price delay shrank as the market matured. Expect post-2020 decay; the Shen et al. result must be re-validated on 2021+ data before sizing.

**Net assessment:** documented Sharpe/hit rates *net of realistic fees* exist only where round-trip ≤ ~5 bps (maker entries, or VIP-tier taker ~2–5 bps). At 20–50 bps taker (typical retail default), the academic edge is fully consumed.

## 2. Intraday momentum in equity indices and gold

**The JFE line:**
- [Gao, Han, Li & Zhou (2018, *JFE* 129(2):394–414)](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0304405X18301351): SPY first half-hour → last half-hour, R² ~2% (4.3% in the crisis), stronger on high-vol/recession/macro-news days, significant in- and out-of-sample. The traded version earns ~6.34%/yr gross (one 30-min trade/day) per the [Alpha Architect summary (practitioner)](https://alphaarchitect.com/2014/08/attention-prop-traders-the-first-half-hour-of-trading-predicts-the-last-half-hour/). That is ~2.5 bps/trade gross over ~250 trades/yr — tiny per trade, but the cost is also tiny (one round trip/day on a ~0.2–0.4% 30-min range).
- [Baltussen, Da, Lammers & Martens (2021, *JFE*)](https://twoquants.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/02/2020_Baltussen_LAmmers_Martens_Intra-day-Momentum.pdf) (see also [Erasmus/Notre Dame listing](https://businessdatascience.nl/publication/168044/hedging-demand-and-market-intraday-momentum)): 60+ futures across equities, bonds, commodities, currencies, 1974–2020. **Rest-of-day return (close→30 min before close) predicts the last 30 min everywhere**, beating the first-half-hour predictor (R²OOS 2.88% vs −1.71% in equities). Traded strategies: **annualized Sharpe 0.87–1.73 at asset-class level, gross**. The effect **reverts over the next 3 days** — transitory price pressure from gamma-hedging of option market-makers and leveraged-ETF rebalancing (mechanism confirmed with negative-gamma-exposure and LETF-market-cap proxies). Currency futures are the weakest class (U-shaped volume least pronounced). Note predictability dies after 16:00 ET even though futures trade to 16:15 — consistent with hedging, not information.
- Global replication: [Li, Sakkas & Urquhart (2022, *J. Financial Markets* 57)](https://ideas.repec.org/a/eee/ecofin/v62y2022ics1062940822000833.html) — intraday TSMOM economically sizable across 16 developed markets.

**Gold/oil:** [Xu, Bouri, Saeed & Wen (2020, *Resources Policy* 69, full text at PMC)](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7480318/): intraday momentum in USO, GLD, SLV — but the predictor half-hour differs by market. For GLD it's the **5th** half-hour (~11:30–12:00 ET), t = 3.03, R² = 0.49%, R²OOS = 0.26%; predictability significant only on high-vol/high-jump days. Crucially, the traded GLD timing strategy's mean is **statistically insignificant (t = 1.23; success rate 53% vs 52% always-long)** — the eye-catching numbers are for GVZ, a volatility index you cannot trade. [Wen, Gong, Ma & Xu (2021, *Economic Modelling* 95)](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0264999319310417) find the standard first-half-hour effect in USO.

**Design implication:** signal window = rest of day; holding period = **last 30 min only**. Multi-hour holds dilute the edge (it reverts over days). Instrument ranking by evidence strength: equity indices > bonds ≈ oil > gold > currencies. Retail CFD viability: on ES futures (~1–2 bps RT) the ~2.5 bps edge is viable; on a gold CFD with ~2–3 bps RT against an unproven GLD edge, it is not. Baltussen's Sharpes are gross and portfolio-level — haircut heavily for a single-instrument retail implementation.

## 3. Session / time-of-day structure in FX and crypto

**Documented in FX (peer-reviewed, mechanisms identified):**
- [Krohn, Mueller & Whelan (2024, *Journal of Finance* 79(1):541–578)](https://ideas.repec.org/a/bla/jfinan/v79y2024i1p541-578.html): the **USD appreciates into the major FX fixes and depreciates afterward** — a pervasive, highly significant W-shaped pattern in the top-9 currencies over 21 years, implying >$1bn/day swings; dealer inventory-risk intermediation of fix demand. Published reference rates *cause* the reversal timing (natural experiments).
- [Melvin & Prins (2015, *J. Financial Markets* 22:50–72)](https://ideas.repec.org/a/eee/reveco/v96y2024ipas1059056024005045.html): equity-hedging flows at the **London 4pm fix** move FX predictably (foreign-equity outperformance → USD demand at fix). [Evans (2018, *JBF* 87:233–247)](https://ideas.repec.org/a/bla/jfinan/v79y2024i1p541-578.html) on WMR-fix trading.
- [Breedon & Ranaldo (2013, *JMCB* 45(5):953–965)](https://ideas.repec.org/a/wly/jmoncb/v45y2013i5p953-965.html): currencies systematically **depreciate during their own local trading hours** (locals are net buyers of FX in their own hours) — a time-of-day effect visible in returns and order flow. [Ranaldo (2009, *JBF* 33(12):2199–2206)](https://ideas.repec.org/a/eee/jbfina/v33y2009i12p2199-2206.html) on segmentation/time-of-day patterns.
- [Elaut, Frömmel & Lampaert (2018, *J. Financial Markets* 37:35–51)](https://ideas.repec.org/a/eee/finmar/v37y2018icp35-51.html): Gao-style intraday momentum in **RUB/USD** around MICEX hours, driven by liquidity provision. Caveat: an exotic pair with a fix-like structure; not evidence for EUR/USD.

**Documented in crypto:**
- [Eross, McGroarty, Urquhart & Wolfe (2019, *RIBF* 49:71–81)](https://centaur.reading.ac.uk/81745/): Bitstamp 5-min data — volume rises through the (GMT) day and falls from ~16:00, i.e., **crypto liquidity/volatility is anchored to US market hours**; confirmed by [Wang, Liu & Hsu (2020)](https://ntnuopen.ntnu.no/ntnu-xmlui/bitstream/handle/11250/3027170/no.ntnu:inspera:116271940:116280635.pdf?sequence=1) (volume/vol higher during US/EU hours, lower on weekends).
- Calendar effects: [Caporale & Plastun (2019, *FRL* 31:258–269)](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1544612318304240) — a **Monday effect exists only in BTC** (not LTC/XRP/Dash), with simulated exploitable profits; [Aharon & Qadan (2019, *FRL* 31:415–424)](https://www.scienpress.com/Upload/JAFB/Vol%2014_4_3.pdf) and [Baur, Cahill, Godfrey & Liu (2019, *FRL* 31:78–92)](https://www.scienpress.com/Upload/JAFB/Vol%2014_4_3.pdf) — day-of-week and time-of-day effects in returns/volume. All pre-2018 samples; small magnitudes; treat as tilts, not signals.

**Folklore verdict:** "London open momentum" and "NY open breakout" in majors have **no direct peer-reviewed documentation** — the real, documented session phenomena are the **fix flows** and the **local-hours depreciation**. [Caporale, Gil-Alana, Plastun & Makarenko (2016, *Computational Economics* 47(2):275–295)](https://ideas.repec.org/a/eee/finlet/v18y2016icp177-183.html) ran a trading robot over intraday anomalies and found they disappear under realistic costs (per the [summary here, practitioner](https://github.com/matthewnyc2/financial_apps/blob/main/OPENING_HOUR_TRADING_RESEARCH.md)).

## 4. Execution alpha from patient (limit-order) entries

- [Harris & Hasbrouck (1996, *JFQA* 31(2):213–231)](https://ideas.repec.org/a/cup/jfinqa/v31y1996i02p213-231_00.html): limit orders placed **at or better than the quote outperform market orders even after penalizing non-execution** — but *unconditional* passive submission is not profitable. Patience helps conditional on a signal; it is not alpha by itself.
- The magnitude, from the [Federal Register's summary of their numbers](https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/FR-1996-06-20/html/96-15448.htm): a buy limit 2% below market nets an average purchase price only **0.2%** better than a market order once non-fills (which force later, worse market orders) are included — i.e., **adverse selection + non-fill erosion consumes ~90% of the nominal price improvement**. That is the key honesty anchor.
- [Cont, Kukanov & Stoikov (2014, *J. Financial Econometrics* 12(1):47–88)](https://arxiv.org/html/2502.07071) (per citation record; see also [smallake.kr PDF](https://www.smallake.kr/wp-content/uploads/2016/05/Kukanov.pdf)): short-horizon price impact is linear in order-flow imbalance with coefficient inversely proportional to book depth. For retail-size orders, impact ≈ 0 — a market order costs ≈ **half the effective spread per leg**, nothing more.
- [Cont & Kukanov (2017, *Quantitative Finance* 17(1):21–39)](https://ideas.repec.org/p/arx/papers/2505.17388.html): optimal placement formalizes the tradeoff — spread capture vs adverse selection and queue risk.

**Verdict on the 1–3 pips/trade claim: not defensible.** What limit entries realistically buy on FX majors: the entry-leg half-spread (~0.1–0.5 pip ECN) minus adverse selection on filled pullback orders, plus non-fill optionality (you skip the setups that run away — a real, if hard-to-book, benefit). On a retail MT4 dealing-desk feed you cannot queue inside the interbank spread at all, so mechanical capture is smaller still. A defensible planning figure is **~0.2–0.5 pip/trade on majors, ≤1 pip on crosses** — worth having at high frequency, irrelevant to whether a strategy lives or dies at 4h+. Do not build the business case on 1–3 pips.

## 5. The cost/bar viability rule

**The formal literature:**
- [Gârleanu & Pedersen (2013, *Journal of Finance* 68(6):2309–2340)](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2511.22766) (citation record): with predictable returns and transaction costs, optimal policy trades **partially toward an "aim portfolio"** — slow-decaying alpha justifies turnover; fast-decaying alpha is consumed by costs. This is the horizon principle in one theorem.
- [Frazzini, Israel & Moskowitz (2018, "Trading Costs of Asset Pricing Anomalies", SSRN)](http://spinup-000d1a-wp-offload-media.s3.amazonaws.com/faculty/wp-content/uploads/sites/3/2021/08/Trading-Cost-of-Asset-Pricing-Anomalies.pdf): real institutional costs are an **order of magnitude below prior academic estimates**, and **small turnover-reducing changes (trade less aggressively, tolerate tracking error) materially raise net returns and break-even capacity**. Momentum survives their costs; high-turnover signals are the vulnerable ones.
- [Novy-Marx & Velikov (2016, *RFS* 29(1):104–147)](https://ideas.repec.org/a/oup/rfinst/v29y2016i1p104-147..html): anomalies with **<50% turnover/month** mostly survive costs with mitigation (a buy/hold spread — stricter entry than exit — is the single most effective technique); **few with higher turnover do**. [DeMiguel, Martin-Utrera, Nogales & Uppal (2020, *RFS* 33(5):2180–2222)](https://lbsresearch.london.edu/id/eprint/3676/1/SSRN-id3392875%20(1).pdf): many published characteristics die entirely after costs. In FX specifically: [Filippou, Maurer, Pezzo & Taylor (2024, *JFE* 159)](https://ideas.repec.org/a/bla/jfinan/v79y2024i1p541-578.html) on transaction costs in FX allocation.
- FX intraday confirmation: [Frömmel & Lampaert (2016, *FRL* 18:177–183)](https://ideas.repec.org/a/eee/finlet/v18y2016icp177-183.html) — 8,000+ technical rules on RUB/USD: higher-frequency shows more *gross* profit, and **real (not estimated) transaction costs wipe out most of it**. [Olson (2004, *JBF* 28(1):85–105)](https://ideas.repec.org/r/eee/jbfina/v28y2004i1p85-105.html): FX rule profits have declined over time. [Schulmeister (2009, *Rev. Financial Economics* 18(4):190–201)](https://ideas.repec.org/a/wly/revfec/v18y2009i4p190-201.html): 2,580 models on 30-min S&P data earn 7.2%/yr **gross** (1983–2007) with declining recent performance. [Bajgrowicz & Scaillet](https://ideas.repec.org/a/eee/empfin/v15y2008i2p199-210.html): most technical-rule "discoveries" are data-snooping false positives.

**Derived rule of thumb (my synthesis — flag as derivation, not a published constant):** the best-documented sub-daily edges are **~2–10 bps gross per trade** (Gao ≈ 2.5 bps; Shen et al. ≈ 3–10 bps breakeven). A momentum signal at horizon h therefore has a chance only if **round-trip cost ≤ ~2–5 bps and ≤ ~5–10% of the h-bar range** (so the edge, a fraction of range, exceeds cost). Applying it:

| Instrument / horizon | Cost per bar (your figures; gold/SPY mine) | Verdict |
|---|---|---|
| FX majors 15m | ~17% | Dead — no documented edge within 2× of this |
| FX majors 1h | ~8% | Dead for generic momentum; only fix-flow trades (~1 pip cost vs 5–10 pip documented moves) plausibly clear it |
| FX 4h / daily | 1–3% | Viable — daily momentum/carry literature survives costs |
| BTC 1h | ~5% at 2.5 bps RT (maker/VIP taker); ~20–40% at default 10–20 bps taker | Viable **only** at low fee tiers; the documented 3–10 bps edge demands it |
| Gold 1h CFD | ~10–15% (spread ~2–3 bps RT vs ~15–20 bps 1h range) | Marginal at best — and GLD's traded effect was insignificant anyway |
| SPY/ES last-30-min | ~1–2 bps RT (futures) vs ~2.5 bps edge | Viable on futures; marginal on CFD spreads |

## Ranked, testable candidate list (for pre-registered backtests with honest costs)

1. **BTC + ETH, US-close momentum (signal: rest-of-day or first-half-hour + reversed second-to-last-half-hour; hold 16:30→17:00 ET), limit entries, ≤5 bps RT venue.** Evidence: moderate — two peer-reviewed papers ([Shen et al. 2022, *Financial Review*](https://centaur.reading.ac.uk/100181/3/21Sep2021Bitcoin%20Intraday%20Time-Series%20Momentum.R2.pdf); [Wen et al. 2022, NAJEF](https://ideas.repec.org/a/eee/ecofin/v62y2022ics1062940822000833.html)) with identified mechanism; gross edge 3–10 bps/trade. Pre-register on 2021+ OOS with your actual fee tier; kill criterion pre-set at net Sharpe ≤ 0.
2. **ES (and secondarily SPY ETF), rest-of-day → last-30-min momentum, futures only.** Evidence: strongest — [Gao et al. 2018 JFE](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0304405X18301351), [Baltussen et al. 2021 JFE](https://twoquants.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/02/2020_Baltussen_LAmmers_Martens_Intra-day-Momentum.pdf) (Sharpe 0.87–1.73 gross, 60+ futures, 45 years), [Li/Sakkas/Urquhart 2022](https://ideas.repec.org/a/eee/ecofin/v62y2022ics1062940822000833.html). Watch: crowding, single-instrument haircut, and that reported Sharpes are portfolio-level gross.
3. **USD fix-flow trade: short USD basket into 16:00 London, exit +1–2h (mimic dealer inventory provision).** Evidence: strong and recent — [Krohn/Mueller/Whelan 2024 JF](https://ideas.repec.org/a/bla/jfinan/v79y2024i1p541-578.html), [Melvin & Prins 2015](https://ideas.repec.org/a/eee/reveco/v96y2024ipas1059056024005045.html), [Evans 2018](https://ideas.repec.org/a/bla/jfinan/v79y2024i1p541-578.html). Retail risk: spread widening in the fix window — must be modeled explicitly; untested at retail.
4. **Crypto daily/weekly time-series momentum + MA ensemble (the low-frequency anchor of the book).** Evidence: strong — [Liu & Tsyvinski 2021 RFS](https://ideas.repec.org/a/oup/rfinst/v34y2021i6p2689-2727..html), [Hudson & Urquhart 2021](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10479-019-03357-1), [Gerritsen et al. 2020](https://ideas.repec.org/a/eee/finlet/v35y2020ics1544612319308025.html), and costs favor daily over 1-min ([Svogun & Bazán-Palomino 2022](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S1042443122000816)).
5. **FX daily (4h–1D) momentum/carry.** Established; cost/bar 1–3% clears the viability rule ([Filippou et al. 2024 JFE](https://ideas.repec.org/a/bla/jfinan/v79y2024i1p541-578.html) for cost discipline in FX).
6. **Weak/conditional (tilts, not standalone signals):** BTC Monday effect ([Caporale & Plastun 2019](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1544612318304240) — pre-2018, likely decayed); session-vol timing for *execution* ([Eross et al. 2019](https://centaur.reading.ac.uk/81745/); [Breedon & Ranaldo 2013](https://ideas.repec.org/a/wly/jmoncb/v45y2013i5p953-965.html)); GLD 5th-half-hour predictor ([Xu et al. 2020](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7480318/) — standalone timing mean insignificant).
7. **Execution overlay, all strategies:** limit/pullback entries for ~0.2–0.5 pip/trade and non-fill optionality ([Harris & Hasbrouck 1996](https://ideas.repec.org/a/cup/jfinqa/v31y1996i02p213-231_00.html); [Fed. Register quantification](https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/FR-1996-06-20/html/96-15448.htm)); buy/hold asymmetry à la [Novy-Marx & Velikov 2016](https://ideas.repec.org/a/oup/rfinst/v29y2016i1p104-147..html).

## Decline explicitly (no credible evidence)

- **ICT / "smart money concepts" (order blocks, FVGs, liquidity sweeps):** zero peer-reviewed literature; only vendor/course material ([HowToTrade PDF](https://howtotrade.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/07/14-Most-Important-ICT-Concepts.pdf), [GhostTraders PDF](https://ghosttraders.co/wp-content/uploads/2022/08/SMART-MONEY-TRADING-GUIDE.pdf) — practitioner). The adjacent phenomenon that *is* documented — stop-loss clustering accelerating trends — is [Osler (2003, *JF*)](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/5051205_Currency_Orders_and_Exchange-Rate_Dynamics_Explaining_the_Success_of_Technical_Analysis) and [Osler (2005, *JIMF* 24(2):219–241)](https://ideas.repec.org/a/eee/jimfin/v24y2005i2p219-241.html); if the team wants "liquidity sweep" logic, rebuild it from Osler's order-clustering evidence, not ICT nomenclature.
- **15m FX scalping EAs:** contradicted by cost arithmetic (17% cost/bar) plus [Frömmel & Lampaert 2016](https://ideas.repec.org/a/eee/finlet/v18y2016icp177-183.html), [Olson 2004](https://ideas.repec.org/r/eee/jbfina/v28y2004i1p85-105.html), [Bajgrowicz & Scaillet](https://ideas.repec.org/a/eee/empfin/v15y2008i2p199-210.html), and the day-trading account-level literature you already have.
- **Opening-range breakouts:** one favorable paper — [Holmberg, Lönnbark & Lundström (2013, *FRL*)](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1544612312000438) — crude oil only, daily OHLC, no cost analysis, no broad replication. Insufficient.
- **"London open" momentum / generic intraday chart patterns:** no peer-reviewed documentation found; intraday anomalies broadly die under costs ([Caporale et al. 2016](https://ideas.repec.org/a/eee/finlet/v18y2016icp177-183.html)).

**Cross-cutting caveats for the pre-registration:** publication bias (Harvey–Liu–Zhu t>3 bar), post-publication decay, gross-vs-net gaps in every traded strategy above, and samples that mostly end pre-2021 — every candidate should be validated out-of-sample on 2021+ data before capital.
